Earthquake Warning System

Earthquake warning systems detect seismic activity and send alerts before strong shaking occurs. Using sensors and GPS data, they provide crucial seconds to minutes for safety measures, helping reduce damage and save lives.

Seismometer

  • Detects Ground Motion: A seismometer measures vibrations caused by seismic waves during an earthquake.
  • Records Earthquake Data: It helps determine the earthquake's magnitude, depth, and location.
  • Supports Early Warning Systems: Seismometers provide real-time data to issue alerts before strong shaking occurs.
  • Used in Research & Monitoring: Scientists use seismometers to study tectonic activity and predict future earthquakes.

Earthquake Detecting Sensor

  • Seismic Detection: The device continuously monitors ground motion to detect early seismic activity.
  • Real-Time Alerts: It provides warnings before strong shaking occurs, allowing people to take precautions.
  • Data Analysis: The system analyzes seismic waves to estimate the earthquake's intensity and impact.
  • Disaster Mitigation: Helps reduce damage and casualties by enabling quick response measures.
