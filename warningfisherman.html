Fisher Man Warning

A Fisher Man Warning is designed to detect, predict, and alert communities about potential flooding. Modern systems use IoT, AI, and GIS for real-time monitoring and accurate forecasting. By providing early warnings, these systems help reduce loss of life, property damage, and economic impact, ensuring better preparedness and response to floods. It consists of data collection through sensors and weather stations, analysis using hydrological models, and dissemination of warnings via SMS, sirens, and media.

Flood Detection device

Rainfall detection device

  • Rainfall Measurement: Uses radar, laser, or optical sensors to track raindrop size, intensity, and rate.
  • Wireless Data Transmission: Sends real-time rainfall data to meteorological centers for analysis.
  • Early Warning System Integration: Helps predict heavy rainfall events and issue alerts.
  • Automated Alerts: Helps in early warning and Heavy rainfall risk assessment.

Deep-Sea Tsunami Sensor

  • Detects Seafloor Pressure Changes: Measures sudden shifts in ocean pressure that indicate a tsunami.
  • Real-Time Data Transmission: Sends data to surface buoys, which relay information to satellites and warning centers.
  • Early Warning System: Helps issue timely tsunami alerts, allowing coastal areas to prepare and evacuate.
  • Durable & Long-Lasting: Designed to withstand extreme underwater conditions for continuous monitoring.
