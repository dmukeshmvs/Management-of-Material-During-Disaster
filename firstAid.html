Precautions to Take During Disasters

Following proper safety measures during disasters can prevent injury and save lives. Here are some essential precautions:

  • Stay calm and avoid panicking.
  • Follow official instructions from authorities and emergency services.
  • Find safe shelter and stay indoors during extreme weather conditions.
  • Keep emergency supplies like food, water, and first aid kits handy.
  • Turn off electricity, gas, and water if instructed to do so.
  • Stay away from windows, doors, and unstable structures.
  • Use flashlights instead of candles to prevent fire hazards.
  • Evacuate if required and follow designated escape routes.
  • Have an emergency contact list and inform loved ones of your status.

Essential Items for an Emergency Supply Kit

Prepare an emergency supply kit with these essential items to sustain yourself during disasters:

  • At least three daysâ€™ supply of water (one gallon per person per day).
  • Non-perishable food items such as canned goods, protein bars, and dried fruits.
  • Battery-powered or hand-crank radio with extra batteries.
  • Flashlight and extra batteries.
  • First aid kit with essential medical supplies.
  • Whistle to signal for help.
  • Dust masks to help filter contaminated air.
  • Plastic sheeting and duct tape for sheltering in place.
  • Wrench or pliers to turn off utilities.
  • Local maps and important contact information.
  • Extra clothing, blankets, and personal hygiene products.
