Cyclone Warning System

A Cyclone Warning System is designed to detect and forecast cyclones using satellites, radar, and weather buoys. It provides early warnings to coastal regions, helping authorities and residents prepare for strong winds, heavy rainfall, and storm surges. These systems issue alerts through radio, television, mobile networks, and sirens, minimizing loss of life and property by enabling timely evacuations and disaster management.

Satellite

  • Cloud Observation: Satellites capture images of storm formations, helping meteorologists identify developing cyclones.
  • Wind and Temperature Monitoring: They measure wind speeds, atmospheric pressure, and sea surface temperatures, which influence cyclone strength.
  • Real-Time Tracking: Satellites continuously monitor cyclone movement, providing updates on direction and intensity.
  • Data for Early Warnings: Information from satellites is used to issue early warnings, helping authorities prepare and evacuate affected areas.
