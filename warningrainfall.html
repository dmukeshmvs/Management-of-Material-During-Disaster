Heavy Rainfall Systems

A Heavy Rainfall Warning System helps predict and warn about intense rainfall that may cause floods and landslides. It collects data from weather radars, satellites, and rain gauges, analyzes it using meteorological models, and sends alerts via SMS, sirens, and media. These warnings enable early preparedness, reducing risks and damages. Modern systems use AI and IoT for real-time monitoring and improved accuracy.

Satellite

  • Real-Time Monitoring: Satellites track rainfall intensity and cloud formation globally.
  • Early Warning System: Help predict storms and issue alerts for heavy rainfall.
  • Disaster Management: Support flood forecasting and emergency response planning.
  • Notable Satellites: NASA's GPM and GOES satellites are widely used for monitoring precipitation.
